Unagi Sauce

Description

Unagi sauce is a thickened mix of soy sauce, sake, mirin and sugar. Traditionally used on grilled eel, it is a great source of sweetness and umami flavour in any dish. It is also incredibly easy to make with mostly easily in Europe available ingredients (mirin can be a bit difficult to come by in supermarkets).

This recipe is taken from Just One Cookbook.

Ingredients

  • 180 ml mirin (If unavailable, can be substituted with 135ml sake and 45ml sugar.)
  • 4.5 Tbsp sake
  • 7.5 Tbsp sugar
  • 180 ml soy sauce

Steps

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. In a small saucepan, add 60 ml mirin, 1½ Tbsp sake, and 2½ Tbsp sugar. Turn on the heat to medium and whisk all the ingredients together.
  3. Then, add 60 ml soy sauce and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and continue simmering for 10 minutes. Toward the end of cooking, you will see more bubbles.
  4. Turn off the heat and let it cool. The sauce will thicken as it cools. It's now ready to use.
